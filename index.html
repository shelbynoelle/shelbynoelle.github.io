ABOUT ME

Do you ever question what beauty is?

Shelby does, and has taken to art as a way for people to think about and hopefully discuss the problems that standards of beauty create.

Shelby Berryann


She hopes her cartoonlike and satirical take on the human body parts typically considered sexy will allow viewers to gain an appreciation for what it is like to have breasts and the ugly reality of life in an age where we are drowning in advertisements and media.

"I really would like to give girls something they can relate to. Our chests determine our worth in the eyes of some people and hopefully these pieces will destroy any preconceived notions about what breasts should look like."

Shelby has worked with oil painting, acrylic painting, watercolor, figure drawing, sculpture, and 3D printing classes. Her interests expand beyond a wide range of mediums, however her preferred method is acrylic painting.

Shelby grew up in Quakertown, Pennsylvania with her small family. She always found refuge in art whenever trying to express herself and was involved in classes, camps and more from an early age. Shelby is in the process of completing her BA in Art at the Pennsylvania State University in University Park, with a primary focus on painting and sculpture.
